mechanics

With the increasing risk of fire in many facilities, relying on fire extinguishing systems has now become an indispensable necessity.
Therefore, many people are looking for the best fire extinguishing systems companies due to the important role they play in securing facilities and buildings by providing advanced solutions to control fires and prevent their spread.
CM contributes significantly to the protection of lives and property by designing, installing, and maintaining effective systems that ensure rapid and effective intervention in the event of a fire.
Our company supplies and installs all types of fire pumps which are considered the vital part of the water fire extinguishing system as they feed the sprinkler network and the fire box network with the appropriate pressure.
The pumps must be drawn from a fixed water source (water tank) whose size is determined based on engineering design and hydraulic calculations.
The fire pump group consists of:
• Electric pump: The electric pump works when the pressure drops below the permissible limit, so it provides the required water flow and appropriate pressure for the network and is of the centrifugal pump type.
• Diesel pump: The diesel pump has the same specifications as the electric pump and works when the power is cut off to do the same work as the electric pump by providing the required water flow and appropriate pressure for the network.
• Jokey pump: The jokey pump is a pressure compensation pump, and it works when the pressure is lost by a small percentage and provides a flow of 10 to 15% of the fire network flow and provides high pressure and is usually of the multistage centrifugal pump type and its purpose is to compensate for any loss in the network resulting from leakage.
Conditions required for fire pumps:
• The pump should give us a flow rate of up to 150% of the required flow rate and at this value it gives us a pressure value of not less than 65% of the designed pressure value.
• The pumps are of the end suction or split case type.
• Each pump has its own plate.
• It is preferable that the pumps are UL & FM certified.
• The pumps must comply with NFPA specifications.
